General Information

Hot water coils are a heat exchanging system that transfers heat to air streams. These coils are crucial for a fully functional heating system in any standard building. Heat is conducted from water flowing through coils as they pass through sheets of formed fins. Heat is transferred from the hot water flowing through the tubes to the cold air, heating a room.

We are pleased to build custom-designed replacement hot water coils for any type of commercial or large residential need. Hot Water Coil Design

Hot water coils heat cold air to provide warm air in a enclosed location. These coils can be used for a variety of purposes ranging from comfort heating to increasing air temperature in duct systems and fluid process heating.

While we receive inquiries on any type of coil, the below tend to be the most common hot water coil design specs.

Rows
Hot water coils typically operate at high temperatures (rangining from 120-180 degrees Fahrenheit). It is common to see one or two row coils.

Tube Diameter

The most common selection for a hot water coil is a 5/8 inch diameter. This sized tube lessens fluid pressure drop while maintaining heat transfer. A 5/8 inch tube also has various wall thickness options, which can increase coils lifespan and prevent tube erosion.
Connections
Hot water coil connections are made of copper and are usually MPT (Male Pipe Thread). Smaller coils may use copper sweat connections. Grooved connection types are becoming increasingly popular, and are available upon special request.
